## Parts of Speech

### 1. Nouns
Words that name people, places, things, or ideas.

**Types:**
- **Common nouns**: book, city, teacher
- **Proper nouns**: London, John, Microsoft
- **Countable**: apple, car, student (can be counted)
- **Uncountable**: water, information, happiness (cannot be counted)

**Examples:**
- The **cat** is sleeping on the **sofa**.
- **Sarah** works at **Google** in **California**.
- I need some **water** and **information** about the project.

### 2. Pronouns
Replace nouns to avoid repetition.

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| Subject | I, you, he, she, it, we, they |
| Object | me, you, him, her, it, us, them |
| Possessive | mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs |
| Reflexive | myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, themselves |

**Examples:**
- **She** gave **me** her book.
- This pen is **mine**, not **yours**.
- I can do it **myself**.

### 3. Verbs
Express actions or states of being.

**Examples:**
- I **run** every morning. (action)
- She **is** a teacher. (state of being)
- They **have** three children. (possession)

### 4. Adjectives
Describe nouns.

**Examples:**
- A **beautiful** garden
- The **tall** building
- **Three** books (quantity)
- An **interesting** story

**Order of adjectives:** Opinion → Size → Age → Shape → Color → Origin → Material → Purpose
- A **beautiful large old round red Italian wooden dining** table

### 5. Adverbs
Modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.

**Examples:**
- She runs **quickly**. (how)
- He will arrive **tomorrow**. (when)
- They live **nearby**. (where)
- I **completely** agree. (degree)

### 6. Prepositions
Show relationships between words.

**Common prepositions:** in, on, at, by, with, from, to, for, about, under, over, between, among

**Examples:**
- The book is **on** the table.
- I'll meet you **at** 5 PM.
- She walked **through** the park.

### 7. Conjunctions
Connect words, phrases, or clauses.

**Types:**
- **Coordinating**: and, but, or, nor, for, yet, so
- **Subordinating**: because, although, if, when, while, unless

**Examples:**
- I like tea **and** coffee.
- She was tired **but** happy.
- I stayed home **because** it was raining.

### 8. Interjections
Express emotions or reactions.

**Examples:**
- **Wow!** That's amazing!
- **Ouch!** That hurts!
- **Hey!** Wait for me!

## Sentence Structure

### Basic Sentence Patterns

1. **Subject + Verb**
   - Birds fly.
   - She laughs.

2. **Subject + Verb + Object**
   - I eat pizza.
   - They play football.

3. **Subject + Verb + Complement**
   - He is happy.
   - She became a doctor.

4. **Subject + Verb + Indirect Object + Direct Object**
   - I gave **her** **a gift**.
   - He sent **me** **an email**.

5. **Subject + Verb + Object + Complement**
   - They elected **him** **president**.
   - We painted **the wall** **blue**.

### Types of Sentences

| Type | Purpose | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Declarative | Make a statement | I love reading books. |
| Interrogative | Ask a question | Do you like coffee? |
| Imperative | Give a command | Close the door. |
| Exclamatory | Show strong emotion | What a beautiful day! |

## Tenses

### Present Tenses

#### 1. Simple Present
**Form:** base verb (add -s/-es for 3rd person singular)
**Use:** Habits, facts, general truths
- I **work** every day.
- She **teaches** English.
- The sun **rises** in the east.

#### 2. Present Continuous
**Form:** am/is/are + verb-ing
**Use:** Actions happening now, temporary situations
- I **am studying** right now.
- They **are playing** football.
- She **is working** on a project this week.

#### 3. Present Perfect
**Form:** has/have + past participle
**Use:** Actions completed at an unspecified time, experiences, continuing situations
- I **have visited** Paris three times.
- She **has lived** here for five years.
- We **have finished** our homework.

#### 4. Present Perfect Continuous
**Form:** has/have + been + verb-ing
**Use:** Actions that started in the past and continue to the present
- I **have been studying** for three hours.
- She **has been working** here since 2020.

### Past Tenses

#### 1. Simple Past
**Form:** verb + -ed (regular) or irregular form
**Use:** Completed actions in the past
- I **worked** yesterday.
- She **went** to London last year.
- They **studied** all night.

#### 2. Past Continuous
**Form:** was/were + verb-ing
**Use:** Actions in progress at a specific time in the past
- I **was sleeping** when you called.
- They **were playing** at 6 PM.
- While I **was cooking**, he **was reading**.

#### 3. Past Perfect
**Form:** had + past participle
**Use:** Actions completed before another past action
- I **had finished** dinner before he arrived.
- She **had already left** when I got there.

#### 4. Past Perfect Continuous
**Form:** had + been + verb-ing
**Use:** Duration of an action before another past action
- I **had been waiting** for two hours when the bus finally came.
- She was tired because she **had been working** all day.

### Future Tenses

#### 1. Simple Future (will)
**Form:** will + base verb
**Use:** Predictions, spontaneous decisions, promises
- I **will help** you tomorrow.
- It **will rain** later.
- She **will be** here soon.

#### 2. Going to Future
**Form:** am/is/are + going to + base verb
**Use:** Plans, intentions, predictions based on evidence
- I **am going to visit** my parents next week.
- Look at those clouds! It **is going to rain**.

#### 3. Future Continuous
**Form:** will be + verb-ing
**Use:** Actions in progress at a specific time in the future
- This time tomorrow, I **will be flying** to New York.
- They **will be waiting** for us at the airport.

#### 4. Future Perfect
**Form:** will have + past participle
**Use:** Actions completed before a specific time in the future
- By next month, I **will have finished** this project.
- She **will have graduated** by June.

## Articles

### Definite Article: THE
**Use:**
- Specific items: **The** book on the table
- Unique items: **the** sun, **the** moon, **the** President
- Superlatives: **the** best, **the** fastest
- Musical instruments: play **the** piano
- Oceans, rivers, mountain ranges: **the** Pacific Ocean, **the** Himalayas

### Indefinite Articles: A / AN
**Use:**
- First mention: I saw **a** dog.
- Jobs: She is **an** engineer.
- One item: I need **a** pen.

**A vs AN:**
- **A** before consonant sounds: **a** book, **a** university (sounds like "you-ni")
- **AN** before vowel sounds: **an** apple, **an** hour (silent 'h')

### No Article (Zero Article)
**Use:**
- General plural nouns: **Cats** are animals.
- Uncountable nouns in general: **Water** is essential.
- Names: **John** lives in **London**.
- Meals: I had **breakfast** at 8 AM.
- Languages: I speak **English**.

**Examples:**
- I bought **a** car. **The** car is red. (first mention → specific reference)
- **Dogs** are loyal. (general)
- **The** dogs in my neighborhood are noisy. (specific)

## Prepositions

### Time Prepositions

| Preposition | Use | Examples |
|-------------|-----|----------|
| **at** | Specific times | at 5 PM, at noon, at midnight, at night |
| **on** | Days and dates | on Monday, on July 4th, on my birthday |
| **in** | Months, years, seasons, parts of day | in January, in 2024, in summer, in the morning |

**Examples:**
- The meeting is **at** 3 PM **on** Friday **in** December.
- I was born **in** 1990 **on** May 15th **at** 2 AM.

### Place Prepositions

| Preposition | Use | Examples |
|-------------|-----|----------|
| **at** | Specific points | at the station, at home, at work, at the door |
| **on** | Surfaces | on the table, on the wall, on the floor |
| **in** | Enclosed spaces | in the room, in the box, in the city, in the car |

**Examples:**
- The book is **on** the table **in** the living room.
- I'll meet you **at** the coffee shop.
- He lives **in** London **on** Baker Street.

### Movement Prepositions

- **to**: I'm going **to** school.
- **from**: She came **from** Paris.
- **into**: He walked **into** the room.
- **out of**: They ran **out of** the building.
- **through**: We drove **through** the tunnel.
- **across**: She swam **across** the river.
- **along**: Walk **along** this street.

### Other Important Prepositions

- **by**: Travel **by** car, finished **by** 5 PM
- **with**: Coffee **with** milk, come **with** me
- **for**: A gift **for** you, waiting **for** an hour
- **about**: Talk **about** the weather
- **between**: **Between** you and me (two items)
- **among**: **Among** the crowd (more than two)

## Modal Verbs

Modal verbs express ability, possibility, permission, obligation, etc.

| Modal | Use | Examples |
|-------|-----|----------|
| **can** | Ability, permission | I **can** swim. **Can** I help you? |
| **could** | Past ability, polite request | I **could** run fast when I was young. **Could** you help me? |
| **may** | Permission, possibility | **May** I come in? It **may** rain. |
| **might** | Possibility (less certain) | I **might** go to the party. |
| **must** | Strong obligation, certainty | You **must** wear a seatbelt. He **must** be tired. |
| **have to** | Obligation | I **have to** work tomorrow. |
| **should** | Advice, recommendation | You **should** see a doctor. |
| **ought to** | Advice (similar to should) | You **ought to** apologize. |
| **would** | Polite requests, habits in past | **Would** you like some tea? I **would** play here as a child. |
| **will** | Future, promises, offers | I **will** call you later. **Will** you marry me? |

**Examples:**
- You **must** arrive on time. (obligation)
- She **can** speak three languages. (ability)
- It **might** snow tomorrow. (possibility)
- **Should** I bring anything? (asking for advice)
- **Would** you mind closing the window? (polite request)

## Conditionals

### Zero Conditional (General Truths)
**Form:** If + present simple, present simple
**Use:** Facts, scientific truths

- **If** you heat water to 100°C, it **boils**.
- **If** it rains, the ground **gets** wet.

### First Conditional (Real Future Possibility)
**Form:** If + present simple, will + base verb
**Use:** Likely future situations

- **If** it rains tomorrow, I **will stay** home.
- **If** you study hard, you **will pass** the exam.

### Second Conditional (Unreal Present)
**Form:** If + past simple, would + base verb
**Use:** Hypothetical or unlikely present/future situations

- **If** I won the lottery, I **would travel** the world.
- **If** I were you, I **would apologize**. (Note: use "were" for all persons)

### Third Conditional (Unreal Past)
**Form:** If + past perfect, would have + past participle
**Use:** Imaginary situations in the past

- **If** I had studied harder, I **would have passed** the exam.
- **If** she had arrived earlier, she **would have met** him.

### Mixed Conditionals
Combine different time periods.

- **If** I had accepted that job (past), I **would be** rich now (present).
- **If** I were taller (present), I **would have become** a basketball player (past).

## Active vs Passive Voice

### Active Voice
**Form:** Subject + Verb + Object
**Focus:** Who does the action

- The chef **cooks** the meal.
- Scientists **discovered** the vaccine.

### Passive Voice
**Form:** Object + be + past participle (+ by + agent)
**Focus:** What happens or what receives the action

- The meal **is cooked** by the chef.
- The vaccine **was discovered** by scientists.

### When to Use Passive Voice
1. When the doer is unknown: My car **was stolen**.
2. When the doer is obvious: The thief **was arrested**.
3. When the action is more important: The bridge **was built** in 1950.
4. In formal/scientific writing: The experiment **was conducted** carefully.

### Passive Voice in Different Tenses

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Present Simple | He writes the letter. | The letter **is written** by him. |
| Present Continuous | He is writing the letter. | The letter **is being written** by him. |
| Past Simple | He wrote the letter. | The letter **was written** by him. |
| Past Continuous | He was writing the letter. | The letter **was being written** by him. |
| Present Perfect | He has written the letter. | The letter **has been written** by him. |
| Past Perfect | He had written the letter. | The letter **had been written** by him. |
| Future Simple | He will write the letter. | The letter **will be written** by him. |

## Direct and Indirect Speech

### Direct Speech
Exact words someone said, in quotation marks.
- She said, **"I am tired."**
- He asked, **"Where do you live?"**

### Indirect Speech (Reported Speech)
Reporting what someone said without exact words.
- She said **(that) she was tired**.
- He asked **where I lived**.

### Changes in Indirect Speech

#### Pronoun Changes
- "I am happy" → He said **(that) he was happy**.
- "We will go" → They said **(that) they would go**.

#### Tense Changes (Backshift)

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Present Simple | Past Simple |
| Present Continuous | Past Continuous |
| Present Perfect | Past Perfect |
| Past Simple | Past Perfect |
| Will | Would |
| Can | Could |
| May | Might |

**Examples:**
- "I **work** here." → He said he **worked** there.
- "I **am studying**." → She said she **was studying**.
- "I **have finished**." → He said he **had finished**.
- "I **will come**." → She said she **would come**.

#### Time and Place Changes

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|---------------|-----------------|
| today | that day |
| tomorrow | the next day / the following day |
| yesterday | the day before / the previous day |
| next week | the following week |
| last week | the previous week |
| now | then |
| here | there |
| this | that |

**Examples:**
- "I will see you **tomorrow**." → He said he would see me **the next day**.
- "I'm busy **now**." → She said she was busy **then**.

#### Reporting Questions
- **Yes/No questions**: Use "if" or "whether"
  - "Do you like coffee?" → He asked **if/whether** I liked coffee.
  
- **Wh- questions**: Keep the question word
  - "Where do you live?" → She asked **where** I lived.

#### Reporting Commands/Requests
Use: told/asked/ordered + person + (not) to + infinitive

- "Close the door." → He **told** me **to close** the door.
- "Please help me." → She **asked** me **to help** her.
- "Don't be late." → He **told** me **not to be** late.

## Common Grammar Mistakes

### 1. Subject-Verb Agreement
**Wrong:** She don't like coffee.
**Correct:** She **doesn't** like coffee.

**Wrong:** The students is studying.
**Correct:** The students **are** studying.

### 2. Using Articles
**Wrong:** I need a water.
**Correct:** I need **water**. (uncountable)

**Wrong:** He is teacher.
**Correct:** He is **a** teacher.

### 3. Countable vs Uncountable Nouns
**Wrong:** I have many informations.
**Correct:** I have **much information** / **a lot of information**.

**Wrong:** She gave me an advice.
**Correct:** She gave me **advice** / **a piece of advice**.

### 4. Preposition Errors
**Wrong:** I arrived to home.
**Correct:** I arrived **home** / I arrived **at** my house.

**Wrong:** I am good in English.
**Correct:** I am good **at** English.

**Wrong:** It depends of you.
**Correct:** It depends **on** you.

### 5. Double Negatives
**Wrong:** I don't have nothing.
**Correct:** I don't have **anything** / I have **nothing**.

### 6. Word Order in Questions
**Wrong:** Where you are going?
**Correct:** Where **are you** going?

**Wrong:** What she is doing?
**Correct:** What **is she** doing?

### 7. Missing Auxiliary Verbs
**Wrong:** You like coffee?
**Correct:** **Do** you like coffee?

**Wrong:** She working now?
**Correct:** **Is** she working now?

### 8. Confusing Adjectives and Adverbs
**Wrong:** She speaks English good.
**Correct:** She speaks English **well**. (adverb)

**Wrong:** He drives careful.
**Correct:** He drives **carefully**. (adverb)

### 9. Using "Make" vs "Do"
**Make:** produce, create (make coffee, make a mistake, make a decision)
**Do:** perform, complete (do homework, do exercise, do the dishes)

**Wrong:** I need to make my homework.
**Correct:** I need to **do** my homework.

### 10. "It's" vs "Its"
**It's** = It is / It has
**Its** = possessive

**Wrong:** The dog wagged it's tail.
**Correct:** The dog wagged **its** tail.

### 11. "Your" vs "You're"
**Your** = possessive
**You're** = You are

**Wrong:** Your late.
**Correct:** **You're** late.

### 12. "There", "Their", "They're"
**There** = place
**Their** = possessive
**They're** = They are

**Examples:**
- The book is **there**.
- It's **their** house.
- **They're** coming tomorrow.

### 13. Using "Much" vs "Many"
**Much** = uncountable nouns
**Many** = countable nouns

**Wrong:** How many water do you need?
**Correct:** How **much** water do you need?

**Wrong:** I don't have much friends.
**Correct:** I don't have **many** friends.

### 14. "Less" vs "Fewer"
**Less** = uncountable nouns
**Fewer** = countable nouns

**Wrong:** There are less people today.
**Correct:** There are **fewer** people today.

### 15. Double Subjects
**Wrong:** My friend he is coming.
**Correct:** My friend is coming. / He is coming.

## Tips for Non-Native Speakers

1. **Read regularly** - Read books, articles, and news in English to see grammar in context.

2. **Practice speaking** - Don't be afraid to make mistakes. Practice makes perfect.

3. **Listen actively** - Watch movies, podcasts, and videos to hear natural English.

4. **Write daily** - Keep a journal or write emails to practice grammar structures.

5. **Learn in context** - Study phrases and sentences, not just individual words.

6. **Focus on common mistakes** - Identify patterns in your errors and work on them.

7. **Use grammar in real situations** - Apply what you learn in conversations and writing.

8. **Be patient** - Language learning takes time. Celebrate small improvements.

9. **Ask for feedback** - Don't hesitate to ask native speakers to correct you.

10. **Review regularly** - Revisit grammar rules and practice them consistently.
